Privacy Policy

Last updated: 31 May 2026

The short version. whatsHappening is anonymous. You don't create an account and we never ask who you are. We don't sell your data, we don't use advertising trackers, and your location stays on your device. We collect a small amount of anonymous, EU-hosted usage and crash data to keep the app working and decide what to build next.

whatsHappening ("the app", "we", "us") helps you discover live music, comedy, club nights, theatre and other events happening near you. This policy explains what we collect, why, and the choices you have. It applies to the whatsHappening mobile app on iOS and Android.

What we collect and why

We do not require an account, and we do not collect your name, profile, contacts, or advertising identifiers. The limited data we do process is anonymous and tied only to a random device-level identifier, never to your real-world identity.

Location

If you allow location access, your device's location is used on your device only — to centre the map on where you are. Your coordinates are never sent to our servers and never attached to any analytics or crash event. You can use the app without granting location permission.

One honest caveat: like any internet service, our analytics and crash-reporting providers receive your device's IP address as part of normal network requests, from which an approximate (city-level) location can be inferred. We do not use this to identify or track you, and we do not store precise location.

What we don't do

  • No accounts, profiles, or sign-up — the app is anonymous.
  • No selling or renting of your data to anyone.
  • No advertising networks, ad trackers, data brokers, or IDFA / cross-app tracking.
  • No collection of your search text, contacts, photos, or precise location on our servers.

Who processes data for us

We use a small number of trusted service providers, chosen for privacy and hosted in the EU where possible:

  • Sentry (EU region) — crash and performance monitoring.
  • PostHog (EU region) — privacy-respecting product analytics.
  • Supabase — stores the public events catalogue and any feedback you send.

Event listings themselves come from third-party ticketing sources. When you tap through to buy a ticket, you leave whatsHappening and the destination site's own privacy policy applies.

Data retention

  • Events catalogue: past events are removed within 7 days of the event date.
  • Analytics & crash data: retained by our providers for a limited period in line with their standard retention policies, then deleted.
  • Feedback: kept while we work through it and for a reasonable period afterwards for support continuity.

Your choices

  • Use the app without granting location permission, or revoke it anytime in your device settings.
  • Choose not to send feedback, or send it without including any contact details.
  • Because the app is anonymous and tied only to a random device identifier, there is no account to delete. Reinstalling the app resets the local identifier. If you have questions about data associated with feedback you sent, contact us below.

Data security

Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS). We keep the data we hold to a minimum, which is the strongest protection of all.

Children

whatsHappening is intended for adults and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we'll update this page and the "last updated" date above. Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy.

Help

Support

We're a small team and we read everything.

Fastest way to reach us: tap the feedback button inside the app — it sends your message straight to us. Prefer email? Write to whatshappeningapp00@gmail.com.

Getting help

Whether something's broken, an event looks wrong, or you've got a feature idea, we want to hear it. There are two ways to get in touch:

  • In-app feedback button — the quickest route; no email app required.
  • Email — whatshappeningapp00@gmail.com

What to include

To help us help you faster, it's useful to mention:

  • What you were trying to do and what happened instead.
  • Your device and OS (e.g. iPhone 15, iOS 18).
  • The name of an event or venue, if your message is about a specific listing.

Response time

We aim to reply within a few business days. whatsHappening is anonymous, so if you'd like a response, please include an email address in your message.

Event listings

Events are aggregated from third-party ticketing sources and refreshed daily. If a listing's details (time, price, venue) look off, the source site is the authority — but please tell us so we can investigate the feed.
